## Executive Summary

The business needed a clearer view of what was driving sales performance across customers, products, and markets. Stakeholders needed to understand which package types were contributing most to volume, how concentrated revenue was among top customers, whether pricing was stable, and which regions deserved greater commercial focus.

To solve this, I built an interactive Power BI dashboard that analyzed e-commerce sales data across revenue, units sold, average unit price, customer concentration, and geographic sales distribution. The dashboard converted raw transactional data into an accessible reporting view that could support decisions in sales planning, customer retention, market expansion, and product prioritization.

The analysis highlighted revenue of $105M, 6M units sold, and an average unit price of $17.6 in the summarized dashboard view. It also showed that the top five customers contributed over 60% of total spend, while Bangladesh and India emerged as the strongest-performing markets. These findings pointed to a business with strong commercial traction, but also clear concentration risk and growth opportunities.

A few immediate next steps would be to strengthen retention efforts for top-spending customers, expand commercial focus in high-performing markets, align inventory planning around top-selling package types such as carat and cans, and improve customer mix by growing mid-tier accounts.

## Business Problem

The core business challenge was understanding where performance was strongest and what actions would best support future growth. Stakeholders needed better visibility into sales trends, product contribution, customer dependency, and market-level performance.

Without a centralized dashboard, it was difficult to answer questions such as:

- Which package types are driving the most volume?
- Is revenue performance supported by stable pricing?
- How much of total spend is coming from a small customer group?
- Which markets should receive more sales and growth attention?
- Where should the business focus to improve retention and reduce revenue concentration risk?

This project addressed those questions by building a business-facing reporting solution that made performance easier to monitor and act on.

## Methodology

1. Data preparation  
The sales dataset was cleaned and structured for reporting. Key fields such as revenue, quantity sold, package type, customer, and geography were standardized to support accurate analysis.

2. Data modeling  
The data was modeled in Power BI to enable analysis across time, customers, products, and markets. Core measures were created for revenue, quantity sold, and average unit price.

3. Dashboard development  
An interactive dashboard was built to track:
- revenue
- quantity sold
- average unit price
- monthly sales trend
- top customer spending
- top units sold by package type
- geographic sales distribution

4. Insight generation  
The final step focused on interpreting the results from a business perspective, identifying performance drivers, concentration risks, and market opportunities, then translating those findings into practical stakeholder recommendations.

## Skills

- Analytics: sales analysis, customer concentration analysis, product performance analysis, geographic analysis
- Business communication: dashboard storytelling, recommendation development

## Results & Business Recommendation

Revenue and sales volume were strong in the summarized dashboard view, with revenue at $105M and total quantity sold at 6M units. The average unit price remained at $17.6, suggesting that commercial performance was supported by steady pricing alongside healthy sales activity.

Business recommendation:  
Leadership and commercial stakeholders should continue focusing on demand generation and sales execution while monitoring whether future growth is coming from volume, pricing, or both. This helps support better revenue planning and performance management.

Carat and cans were the top-selling package types, making them the strongest contributors to product volume.

Business recommendation:  
Product, merchandising, and operations stakeholders should prioritize these categories in inventory planning, promotional activity, and supply continuity. Concentrating commercial support around high-performing package types can help protect sales momentum and improve product availability.

The top five customers contributed over 60% of total spend, showing that revenue was heavily concentrated among a small group of accounts.

Business recommendation:  
Sales and account management stakeholders should prioritize customer retention planning, account growth, renewal protection, and revenue diversification. Protecting key accounts is essential, but the business should also reduce concentration risk by expanding engagement with mid-tier and emerging customers.

Bangladesh and India appeared as the strongest-performing markets in the geographic distribution view.

Business recommendation:  
Sales and growth stakeholders should increase market expansion efforts in these regions through targeted campaigns, localized commercial planning, and stronger distribution support. These markets represent the clearest opportunities for deeper penetration and scalable growth.

Overall, the dashboard showed a business with solid commercial performance, but also one that would benefit from a more balanced customer mix and sharper market prioritization.
